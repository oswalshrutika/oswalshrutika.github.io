## 1. 60 → 40 min: Physical & tools setup

- [ ] Water bottle within reach. Take a sip before hard questions.
- [ ] Bathroom now, not later.
- [ ] Phone on Do Not Disturb but reachable (in case rescheduling).
- [ ] Close Slack, email, browser tabs unrelated to interview.
- [ ] If virtual: test camera, mic, screen-share **now**, not at 4:59.
- [ ] Confirm the calendar invite: time zone, meeting link, interviewer name(s).
- [ ] Have the JD open in a tab you can glance at.
- [ ] Notepad + 2 working pens (or a text file open) for notes.
- [ ] A printed copy of your resume within eyeshot.
- [ ] The **00_INDEX.md** open in a browser tab so you can jump.

## 2. 40 → 25 min: Speed-read cheat sheets

**Only these two files (~15 min):**
- [ ] `08/01_cheatsheet_fix_tags.md` — scan the tables; recite tags 11, 35, 37, 39, 41, 55, 54, 150 aloud.
- [ ] `08/02_cheatsheet_order_lifecycle.md` — trace the mermaid diagram once with your finger.

**Then one of these (~10 min), based on role focus:**
- [ ] Equities-heavy → `08/03_cheatsheet_us_market_structure.md`
- [ ] Data / infra bent → `08/04_cheatsheet_sql.md` OR `08/05_cheatsheet_linux.md`
- [ ] Networking-heavy → `08/06_cheatsheet_networking.md`

## 3. 25 → 15 min: STAR rehearsal

- [ ] Open `08/07_cheatsheet_star_openers.md`.
- [ ] Pick **3 stories** (out of 5) you're most confident in.
- [ ] Say each **3-sentence version aloud** twice. Time yourself: goal is < 45 seconds per story.
- [ ] Say aloud one of the 10 opener sentences that fits the role.

## 4. 15 → 5 min: Mental warm-up

- [ ] Open `08/08_flashcards.md`.
- [ ] Skim through 10 random rows. Answer each in your head; don't dwell.
- [ ] Recall the **3 numbers** you will drop as anchors during the interview:
  - Number of years you've supported OMS: **5**.
  - Approx orders/day at peak client: (pick a defensible number, e.g. **~50,000**)
  - Time-to-mitigation on a Sev-2 you closed: (e.g. **~40 minutes**)

## 5. 5 → 0 min: Ready posture

- [ ] Stand up. Two deep breaths. Roll shoulders.
- [ ] Sip water.
- [ ] Warm your voice: read one sentence aloud clearly.
- [ ] Sit up straight; camera at eye level.
- [ ] Smile once before the call connects — the physical setup helps tone.

## 6. In-the-room reminders

- **Anonymize** client/broker names always. "A US buy-side client." "A European sell-side broker."
- **Structure your answer aloud** before diving in: "There are two parts to that — first the session-layer view, then the app-layer."
- **When you don't know:** "I haven't worked with X directly, but the way I would approach it is..." — then reason from principles.
- **Numbers > adjectives.** "Rejects dropped from 40/day to zero" beats "the fix worked well."
- **Own the mistake in the mistake question.** No blaming clients, vendors, or teammates. But do describe the systemic fix you added afterwards.
- **Ask a question at the end.** Prepped list:
  1. What does a typical Sev-1 look like on your team and how does escalation work?
  2. How does the T/A team interact with dev — do you own tickets end-to-end or hand off?
  3. What percent of the role is trader-facing vs deep-technical?
  4. What's the on-call rotation and how are handovers done?
  5. What's the biggest bottleneck in the team's workflow today?
- **Ask about scope**, not just perks. Shows engineering-mindset.

## 7. If you have 2 minutes left in the interview

Interviewer says "any last questions?" — pick one from:
1. "What would success in the first 90 days look like?"
2. "Which of the incidents your team has closed recently would have been the most interesting to work?"

If they say "we have 30 seconds"—
- "Thanks for the time — I'm very interested; can you tell me the next-step timeline?"

## 8. Post-interview

- [ ] Immediately (within 15 min) open your notepad and jot:
  - Names of everyone you spoke with (spelled correctly).
  - 2–3 questions they seemed to weight heavily.
  - Anything you flubbed — you'll want it fresh if you come back for a follow-up round.
- [ ] Thank-you email within 4 hours. One paragraph. Reference one specific thing from the conversation. Keep it under 8 sentences.
- [ ] Do **not** ping the recruiter for a decision for 3 business days.
- [ ] Update `_context/RESUME.md` if you're continuing prep for other loops — capture the lesson.

## Anti-checklist — do NOT do in the last hour

- [ ] ~~Read a fresh comprehensive doc.~~ You'll only muddy recall.
- [ ] ~~Attempt a new hard SQL problem.~~ You'll get stuck and shake your confidence.
- [ ] ~~Skim LinkedIn for the interviewer.~~ You should have done that days ago.
- [ ] ~~Rehearse a story you didn't already pick.~~ You'll deliver it worse.
- [ ] ~~Drink strong coffee if you don't normally.~~ Nerves + caffeine = shaky voice.
- [ ] ~~Eat a heavy meal.~~ Light snack; keep glucose steady.

**Final rule:** you already know what you know. The last hour is *retrieval practice + posture*, not learning.
